Captain Thomas Coram - Founder of world's first incorporated charity.

Captain Thomas Coram (c. 1668 â€“ 29 March 1751) was a philanthropist who created the London Foundling Hospital in Lamb's Conduit Fields, Bloomsbury to look after abandoned children. It is said to be the world's first incorporated charity.

Here's a time-line of Captain Thomas Coram's life:

  • 1668 -Thomas Coram was born in Lyme Regis, Dorset, England..
  • 1694 - He moved to Taunton, Massachusetts.
  • 1703 - He donated 59 acres (24 ha) of land at Taunton to be used for a schoolhouse,
  • 1704 - He settled in London.
  • 1701 - 1714 - He commanded a merchant ship and acquired the epithet of captain.
  • 1717 - He unsuccessfully promoted the idea of founding a colony to be called 'Georgia' in what is today Maine as a philanthropic venture.
  • 1735 - He brought forward a plan for settling unemployed English artisans in Nova Scotia.
  • 1735 - Foundling Hospital was established.
  • 1751 - Thomas Coram died at the age of 83.
